Kvant Test Article (KTA) Project

This is the story of something started in November 2017. An important relationship of my life had ended in a sunny Friday afternoon, and then I started reading about the Space Shuttle in a different outlook. What if the future of Space Exploration was an upgraded version of that incredible machine? The solution I'm developing on my own is called Kvant.



I think the logo I designed for it represents its most essential faces. The K, with its striations, represents the solid acceleration of the vehicle, using its innovative thrusters. The cursor, symbol of focus, goes towards a star, to symbolize the unstoppable flow of exploration. Finally, the moving particle represents the effects of Kvant in scientific research, war strength and economic capacity.

Ikarus-X Development Report

I started coding, sketching and writing scripts, things and notes, during my classes, in the evenings, while playing the guitar. That concept of an hybrid space propulsion: "What if I combined electric propulsion with chemical propulsion?". From that point only the first model, which I had called Ikarus-X, had seen the blue blue sky.

Ikarus-Y Development Model

This is something quite more complex, my first attempt to embrace the concept of SSTO spacecraft. The first idea was to keep the original Ikarus-X design, removing the BLDC propellers and putting something like this stuff, but this way it wouldn't have been original and I'd have burnt some fossil fuel, which is certainly not good for the environment. At that stage I started working on a kind of rocket engine, that I use to call Strike, which requires a lot of electric energy. This is why I left the project unfinished - I needed some kind of energy source that doesn't even exist at the current stage.

Starseeker Modular Telescope

When I was 15, I was member of an astrophile enthusiasts. Inspired by our passion, and frustrated by the quality of the telescopes at school, we decided to design a telescope on our own. A friend of mine would have realised all the optics, thanks to the heaps of aluminum he had as a shipowner. Unfortunately he had an accident, and my girlfriend and I splitted up. At some point, mantaining the old name she chose, Starseeker, I finished the work on the basis of JWST. Here is a model of what I had done.
