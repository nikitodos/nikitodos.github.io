The Space Framework

Giant modular semi-permanent manned bases in interplanetary space and advanced low cost transportation systems...

Stuff of space cadet dreams or a feasible traffic model for space exploration?

...for the purpose of economically moving cargo and personnel to and from the bases.

The Space Framework concept aims to integrate Solar System resources into the terrestrial economy, hastening the transition to a space-oriented economy while enhancing safety and flexibility in spaceflight for exploration.

To achieve these ambitious goals, it is necessary to focus on the development of new generation space stations, capable for advanced technological research and tourism and incorporating shipyard-like facilities, a new vehicle for atmospheric and extra-atmospheric transport of people and cargo, and new facilities on the main celestial bodies of the Solar System, i.e. infrastructures on key celestial bodies for in-situ resource utilization directly in space.

In tandem with technological advancements, parallel efforts in economics and politics are imperative. Current space laws and procedures, while established by the Outer Space Treaty of 1967, lack the robustness necessary to ensure peace within the envisioned framework. Strengthening these legal frameworks and fostering international cooperation are essential for ensuring the program's safety and sustainability for future development.
